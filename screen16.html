Introduction

I've always had the energy and enthusiasm to dive into every aspect of the process, and polish every detail, unfortunately a conventional job doesn't offer that freedom, so now I'm building my own stuff :)

Hi, I'm Elizabeth - I work at the intersection of design, research, product management and marketing.

I'm passionate about creating meaningful avenues to solve real-world problems.

I'm Curious, Adventurous, Ambitious, Tenacious. My interests have an eclectic range: UX to AI, to making music, teaching MMA moves, hiking, community building etc.

I highly value collaboration, leadership, proactivity, open-mindedness, respect, drive, and optimism.

Looking for someone who can handle the technical side.

Pitch

Imagine never worrying about what to cook again. That's what I'm building - an AI-powered meal planner builds weekly menus based on your taste preferences, dietary needs, and budget. It automatically generates shopping lists and even finds local grocery deals to save you time and money while helping you eat healthier.

Who I'm Looking For

I need a technical co-founder who doesn't just write code — someone who thinks in systems, cares deeply about the end user, and gets genuinely excited when a product clicks. You should be the kind of person who reads the design brief and immediately starts imagining the architecture.

Ideally full-stack or backend-heavy, with some experience shipping consumer-facing products. Startup experience is a bonus, but what matters more is that you've built something real, run into walls, and figured your way through anyway.

I value speed, directness, and low ego. I'll push back on you and I'd love for you to push back on me. We're not looking for a yes-person — we're looking for someone who will fight for the right decision and then move fast once it's made.

If you've ever thought "why hasn't anyone solved this yet?" while standing in a supermarket aisle — we'll probably get along just fine.

What Energises Me

My interests are genuinely all over the place — in the best way. I've been producing electronic music since I was 17, mostly ambient and lo-fi stuff I'll probably never release publicly. There's something about building a soundscape from scratch that feels remarkably similar to building a product.

I teach Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu and MMA fundamentals on weekends to a small community group. Martial arts taught me more about staying calm under pressure than anything else I've ever done.

I'm deep into the intersection of AI and human behaviour — I read a lot about cognitive science, decision-making, and how technology nudges us in ways we don't notice. It feeds directly into how I think about product design.

I hike whenever I can drag myself away from a screen. Solo trails mostly — it's where I do my best thinking. Last year I completed a 9-day trek through Ladakh and came back with three new product ideas and one very unreliable knee.

I also care a lot about community. I co-run a small founders circle in Bengaluru — about 40 early-stage builders who meet monthly, share problems, and occasionally save each other from terrible decisions.

What I've Built & Done

I spent three years at a Series B health-tech startup leading product design — took the mobile app from 12K to 400K users, rebuilt the onboarding flow from scratch (4x improvement in activation), and eventually moved into a hybrid PM/design role when the team scaled.

Before that I freelanced across 11 clients over two years — everything from B2B SaaS dashboards to D2C packaging. It wasn't glamorous but it gave me a brutally honest education in what clients actually need versus what they say they want.

I've been building my current venture — an AI-powered meal planning platform — for 14 months. We've done 3 rounds of user research (68 interviews), shipped two pilot versions, ran a 90-person beta, and got accepted into a top-5 Indian startup accelerator. We're at the point where the product vision is clear and validated. What's missing is the technical horsepower to build it at speed.

I'm also a guest lecturer at a design school in Bengaluru, which I find keeps me sharp — nothing exposes gaps in your thinking faster than having to explain it to 22-year-olds who will absolutely call you out.
